# Skill: Screenwriting Frameworks (Technical Encyclopedia)



Comprehensive technical protocols for the structural design and engineering of narratives for film, video, and automated media in the 2025 ecosystem. This document defines the standards for Structural DNA (3-Act, Hero's Journey), beat-level pacing, and the mathematical modeling of tension arcs.

---

## 1. Structural DNA: The Narrative Skeleton
Standardizing on the most resilient frameworks for technical and creative storytelling.

### 1.1 The 3-Act Engineering Standard
*   **Act I (Setup):** Exposition, Inciting Incident, Plot Point 1. (Standard: 25% of total length).
*   **Act II (Confrontation):** Rising Action, Midpoint (The "No-Return" gate), Plot Point 2. (Standard: 50% of total length).
*   **Act III (Resolution):** Pre-climax, Climax, Resolution. (Standard: 25% of total length).

### 1.2 The Hero's Journey (Monomyth) Protocols
*   **Logic:** Utilizing the 12-stage cycle (Call to Adventure, Threshold, Transformation, Return) to create deeply resonant educational narratives.
*   **Technical Mapping:** Mapping "The Call" to a user's technical problem and "The Transformation" to the acquisition of a new skill.

---

## 2. Beat-to-Beat Pacing & Tension Arcs
Modeling the flow of energy throughout the screenwriting process.

### 2.1 The "Beat Sheet" Standard
*   **The Beat:** The smallest unit of story movement—an action/reaction pair that changes the "Value" of the scene (e.g., from +Positive to -Negative).
*   **Pacing Math:** Targeting a major Value Shift every 2-3 minutes in long-form content to prevent narrative stasis.

### 2.2 Tension Wave Theory
Utilizing a "Sawtooth" wave model for technical tutorials:
1.  **Complexity Spike:** Introduction of a difficult concept (High Tension).
2.  **Simplification/Analogy:** Grounding the concept (Release).
3.  **Repeat:** Building towards the Master Synthesis.

---

## 3. Character Archetype Modeling
Designing "Personas" that facilitate learning and emotional connection.

### 3.1 The "Technical Mentor" Archetype
*   **Goal:** To provide the expertise and tools the "Hero" (the Viewer) needs to solve the problem.
*   **Voice & Tone:** Authoritative, empathetic, and strictly objective.

### 3.2 Archetype Consistency Protocols
Ensuring that character behavior never violates the established "Character DNA" manifest (e.g., a "Stoic Architect" persona should not use slang).

## 6. Glossary of Screenwriting Terms
*   **Exposition:** The introduction of background information.
*   **Inciting Incident:** The event that starts the story's main action.
*   **Climax:** The point of highest tension and the turning point.
*   **Plot Point:** A significant event that turns the story in a new direction.
*   **Subtext:** The underlying meaning or theme beneath the literal dialogue.

---

## 7. Mathematical Foundations: The Golden Section in Acts
*   **Logic:** Placing the "Climax" at the $0.618$ point of the final act to ensure maximum psychological impact.
*   **Timing Math:** Calculating exact page/word counts for each act based on a target duration (1 min = ~130-150 words).

## 8. Troubleshooting & Performance Verification
*   **Flat Arc:** The story lacks tension or movement. *Fix: Introduce a "Shadow" conflict or a competing technical trade-off.*
*   **Information Dump:** Too much exposition in Act I. *Fix: Move 50% of the exposition into the "Rising Action" phase.*
